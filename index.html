Asphodel: 2020

This game jam entry was a team project done in a group of four people. I was the modeling artist for this game, and I built the maze, the props, and the looming God statues that surround the scene, as well as writing all of the voice lines. I used Maya to model all of the assets, and designed them all by hand. The hardest part was refining the God statues so that they were recognizably human, but also so the planes of the face wouldn't make odd shadows in the dim lighting. The game itself is based off of a dream I once had: the player is lost in a maze, taunted by God, and tasked with finding the right random object in order to escape. You only have five minutes to find the right key and get out!

Estherwald: 2017-2018

This castle is the main setting for the introduction of my book. I made it in 3ds Max originally as a reference so when I was writing I could walk through the castle, but deicded to keep it around in case I chose to make my story into a game instead. One of the hardest parts of modeling this was designing it myself: I wanted it to have clear Indian/Russian influences, but I also wanted it to be noticeably "fantasy". The end result is a series of standalone buildings surrounded by a wall, topped with onion domes and with bridged towers interspersed.
